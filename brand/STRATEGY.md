# אבו אמין חלפים — Brand Strategy v1

## Brand Archetype

**Primary: Sage (Expert)**  
*"We give you the insight to decide wisely"*

The Sage archetype is the natural home for a specialist parts dealer. Abu Amin doesn't just sell parts — they know which part fits which car, which brand is worth the extra cost, and which equivalent is just as good. That expert knowledge is the core value proposition. Mechanics drive from Haifa because they trust the recommendation, not just the stock.

**Secondary: Everyman**  
*"We are for everyone, without pretension"*

The Everyman touch prevents the Sage from feeling elite or intimidating. This is a family business on a main road in Isfiya — you walk in, they know your car, they quote a fair price. Not a sterile showroom. Not a call centre. A real shop with real people.

**Why NOT Hero:** The Hero archetype (CAT, DeWalt, Nike) is about conquest and personal achievement. Abu Amin's customers don't want to conquer — they want their car fixed, on budget, today. The brand serves, it doesn't challenge.

**Archetype Blend Rule (from skill methodology):** Primary governs color and shape. Secondary influences typography tone and imagery. Result: structured, expert-feeling visuals (Sage) with approachable, readable, non-intimidating type (Everyman).

## Brand Story

Abu Amin Halafim has been the go-to spare parts address on the Daliyat–Isfiya main road for decades. While big chains built warehouses and call centres, Abu Amin built relationships — with the mechanics who rely on them every day and the drivers who show up with a broken car and no idea what part they need. The answer is always the same: honest advice, OEM and trusted-equivalent brands (Bosch, Valeo, Denso), and fair prices. No upselling. No guesswork. From brake pads to pressure washers, from oils to batteries — if your car needs it, or your garage does, they have it. In a market full of options, Abu Amin's edge is simple: they actually know.

## Tone of Voice

**Register:** Confident, direct, knowledgeable. Speaks as an expert peer, not a salesperson.

**Do:**
- Lead with the specific (part number, model year, brand) — don't be vague
- Use plain Hebrew (לא עברית עסקית — Hebrew, not corporate-speak)
- Acknowledge the mechanic's knowledge — don't talk down
- Be concise: "מצוי · מחיר טוב · יש ערבות" (in stock · good price · warranty)

**Don't:**
- Use marketing superlatives (הכי טוב! המחיר הכי נמוך!)
- Sound like a chain store (automated, impersonal, generic)
- Make it religious or political
- Over-explain what a part does to a mechanic who already knows
